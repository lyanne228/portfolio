Industrial Design

When Mar - May 2017
Where Northwestern University, IL, USA
Main role Student

I took an introductory class on industrial design to learn and practise fast sketching for ideation. Throughout the course, I completed 6 projects and a few short sprints where I incrementally applied new sketching skills.

Each assignment or project was themed, and I chose a specific focus that most interested me for each theme. The goal was to come up with a variety of concepts and ideas, and narrow down these concepts to a final concept drawing.

Hand Tool

The first project was an introduction to shapes and forms, and quick sketching for ideation. I found that once I sketched an idea that I really liked, it was pretty difficult to remove myself from that idea and explore other ideas. Initially, I was hooked onto the mouse-teardrop shaped cutter. Further exploration of ideas led me to my final concept, and I'm glad for it.

Shoes

We worked with Pensole's shoe templates, and were allowed to draw inspiration from anything. I wanted to design shoes that were easy to put on and comfortable. This was inspired by Nike's flyknit material. I particularly love the material and think its the most durable material for the comfort it provides.

I wanted it to be easy to put on because I'm often rushing for everything - tying laces takes time, and all my slip ons are either too informal for the occasion or uncomfortable. The final concept was designed to take less than 5 seconds to wear and be super comfortable.

Camping Experience

I started off this project by talking to several people I know that have gone camping before. Most people that I spoke to enjoyed camping, but hated the process of packing up a campsite in the morning. Packing up involves washing the dishes and pots used the night before, rolling up the sleeping bag, and packing the tent. I thought I could make one of these processes easier, by combining the sleeping bag and tent together, so the camper would only have to pack the tent.

Going with the idea of creating an easy-to-pack tent, I wanted to replace the conventional collapsible pole and tent cover set up, with an inflatable design. I loved the concept of building a tent and sleeping pad by simply pumping air into one corner. I also designed the tent to be modular, so that they could stand alone for a single camper, or be connected to more units for a group.

Furniture

I wasn't interested in designing furniture like a sofa or bed or a chair. I wanted to do something more decorative and fun, so I chose to design floating planters. For me, plants are an essential part of indoor decorating and make any environment feel more lived in. I liked the idea of floating planters because they are different from a conventional pot or vase.

I liked the aesthetic of copper coil embedded in glass (this was my chosen method of levitation), and thought that it added an elegant yet industrial feel to the planter designs. Although this was designed to be a planter, these simple floating containers could hold other kinds of small objects.

Coffee Machine

For my final project, I chose to design coffee machines. My favorite design was inspired by a coffee bean. I like the round curves in a coffee bean, and the natural way that a coffee bean is 'split' into halves fit in with my mental model of a coffee machine - a container for water, another for coffee beans or grounds, and magic.

I designed the ZAP! machine because coffee is my source of energy, so the lightning bolt seemed like an apt metaphor. I also really enjoy the shape of an hourglass. The Coffee Condor was born of this shape. This gave rise to another animal-inspired design - the elephant.
