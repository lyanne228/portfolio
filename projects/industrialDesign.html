Industrial Design

When Mar - May 2017
Where Northwestern University, IL, USA
Main role Student

I took an introductory class on industrial design to learn and practise fast sketching for ideation. Throughout the course, I completed 6 projects and a few short sprints where I incrementally applied new sketching skills.

Each assignment or project was themed, and I chose a specific focus that most interested me for each theme. The goal was to come up with a variety of concepts and ideas, and narrow down these concepts to a final concept drawing.

Hand Tool

The first project was an introduction to shapes and forms, and quick sketching for ideation. I found that once I sketched an idea that I really liked, it was pretty difficult to remove myself from that idea and explore other ideas. Initially, I was hooked onto the mouse-teardrop shaped cutter. Further exploration of ideas led me to my final concept, and I'm glad for it.

Shoes

We worked with Pensole's shoe templates, and were allowed to draw inspiration from anything. I wanted to design shoes that were easy to put on and comfortable. This was inspired by Nike's flyknit material. I particularly love the material and think its the most durable material for the comfort it provides.

I wanted it to be easy to put on because I'm often rushing for everything - tying laces takes time, and all my slip ons are either too informal for the occasion or uncomfortable. The final concept was designed to take less than 5 seconds to wear and be super comfortable.
